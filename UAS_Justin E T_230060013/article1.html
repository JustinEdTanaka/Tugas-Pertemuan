When Blood Vessels Grow Awry

Blood vessels wind all throughout your body. They ensure that blood, and the oxygen it carries, reaches all your body's cells and tissues. Arteries carry oxygen-rich blood from your heart to organs and tissues. Veins carry blood back to your heart to pick up more oxygen. And tiny vessels called capillaries connect the arteries to the veins.

When blood vessels grow abnormally, it can lead to serious health complications. Some common issues include:

  • Atherosclerosis: The buildup of fats and cholesterol in arteries, leading to narrowing and blockage.
  • Aneurysms: Weakening of a blood vessel wall, causing it to bulge or rupture.
  • Varicose veins: Swollen, twisted veins often seen in the legs, caused by faulty valves.

Maintaining healthy blood vessels is crucial for overall health. Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and avoiding tobacco can help keep your blood vessels in good condition.
